CTO Talking Points
  • ▸ Custom = zero wasted features. GHL ships with funnels, landing pages, reputation management — none relevant to PUX. We build only what they need.
  • ▸ The reorder engine is the real ROI. After 3 months of data, it will predict when every retailer is due to reorder and automate outreach. GHL can't do this — it has no order data.
  • ▸ Migration is zero-risk. GHL has clean CSV export. We run both in parallel. No data loss, no hard cutover.
  • ▸ Multi-tenant from day one. PUX team, sales reps, and retailers all get appropriate access — something GHL can't cleanly support for this use case.
  • ▸ WhatsApp stays central. We're not disrupting their retailer relationships — we're pulling WhatsApp into the CRM so no conversation gets lost.
  • ▸ Phased delivery = early value. CRM is live in 2–3 weeks. They don't wait 12 weeks for the full platform to see results.
Likely Objections
"Will we lose our data migrating from GHL?"
No. GHL has clean CSV export. Migration runs parallel — GHL stays live until we've validated every record.
"GHL already has automation, why switch?"
GHL automation is time-based sequences. Ours is behaviour-based — it uses real WooCommerce order history to know exactly when a retailer will run out. Completely different capability.
"Why not just use HubSpot or Pipedrive?"
No wholesale ordering portal, no Freightcom API, no reorder prediction, no multi-tenant retailer access. You'd still need 4–5 separate tools that don't talk to each other.
"£10k seems high just to start"
This isn't just a CRM replacement — it's the foundation of the full platform. Multi-tenant, role-based, WooCommerce-connected, built for 10 stores or 10,000. The architecture scales with PUX.
